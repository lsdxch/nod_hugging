Why We Must Act

Africa's biodiversity faces unprecedented threats. Poaching, habitat loss, and climate change are pushing iconic species to the brink of extinction. If we don't act today, they will be gone tomorrow.

Species in Crisis

Critically Endangered

African Elephant

As land's largest mammals, they are ecosystem engineers but face severe threats from ivory poaching and conflict.

Vulnerable

African Lion

Due to habitat fragmentation and loss of prey, the "King of Beasts" has seen its territory shrink rapidly.

Critically Endangered

Black Rhino

Once driven to the brink by the greed for their horns, conservation efforts are their only hope for survival.
